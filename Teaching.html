At the Materials Modeling Lab (MML), we offer a range of theoretical and practical courses within the Department of Metallurgical Engineering at IIT BHU. Our programs equip students with skills in computational techniques, materials modeling, and metallurgical processes, preparing them for careers in research and industry.

Computational Methods for Metallurgy (MT 561)

This course introduces numerical methods for solving metallurgical engineering problems. Topics include:

  • Error analysis and numerical differentiation/integration
  • Root finding and optimization
  • Finite difference and finite element methods
  • Simulation of heat transfer, solidification, and metal forming

Outcome: Students will develop algorithms to address complex metallurgical challenges, bridging theory and computation.

Modeling and Simulation in Metallurgy (MT 404)

This course explores advanced modeling techniques, including:

  • Mathematical and multi-scale modeling
  • Phase-field methods for microstructures
  • Molecular dynamics, Monte Carlo, and neural networks
  • Phase diagram computation and process modeling

Outcome: Students gain expertise in simulating materials behavior, complementing experimental approaches.

Heat Treatment (MT 304)

This course covers techniques to modify metal and alloy microstructures, focusing on:

  • Iron-carbon equilibrium and transformation diagrams
  • Austenization, grain size, and hardenability
  • Quenching, annealing, tempering, and case hardening
  • Heat treatments for non-ferrous alloys (Al, Cu, Ti)

Outcome: Students learn to correlate heat treatment with material properties for industrial and research applications.

Computer Applications in Metallurgy Practical

This hands-on course teaches software tools for metallurgical analysis, including:

  • CALPHAD software for phase diagram calculations
  • Simulation of thermodynamic and kinetic processes
  • Data analysis with Python and MATLAB

Outcome: Students gain practical skills in computational tools for materials research.

Heat Treatment Practical

This lab-based course focuses on:

  • Performing annealing, quenching, and tempering
  • Microstructural analysis using optical microscopy
  • Hardness testing and property evaluation

Outcome: Students master experimental techniques to optimize alloy performance.

Modeling and Simulation in Metallurgy Practical

This practical course covers:

  • Running molecular dynamics simulations
  • Monte Carlo methods for phase transitions
  • Validating models against experimental data

Outcome: Students develop proficiency in simulation software for materials design.
