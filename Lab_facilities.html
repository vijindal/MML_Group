Lab Facilities

The Materials Modeling Lab (MML) at IIT BHU is equipped with cutting-edge facilities for materials synthesis, processing, and characterization, supporting our research in computational thermodynamics and alloy design.

View Instrument Usage Charges

Computer Server

Specifications:
Processor: Intel Xeon Gold 6140 (2.30GHz, 18-core, 24.75MB, 140W)
RAM: 384 GB
Storage: 8000 GB (NL SAS)
Applications: High-performance computing for DFT calculations, CALPHAD simulations, and machine learning in materials design.

Vacuum Hot Press

Specifications:
Capacity: 20 tons
Max Temperature: 1500°C
Chamber: Controlled atmosphere
Applications: Sintering of metal and ceramic powders, fabrication of Ti-based composites, and diffusion bonding.

Charpy Impact Tester

Specifications:
Capacity: 450 Joules
Applications: Evaluating impact resistance of metallic materials, including high entropy alloys and Ti-composites.

Vacuum Arc Furnace with Suction Casting

Specifications:
Max Temperature: >3500°C
Applications: Melting high-temperature materials like refractory alloys and producing homogeneous samples via suction casting.

High Vacuum High-Temperature Furnace

Specifications:
Max Temperature: 1500°C
Atmosphere: Controlled high vacuum
Applications: Precise heat treatment of alloys, annealing, and phase transformation studies.

Planetary Ball Mill

Specifications:
Speed: Up to 300 RPM
Modes: Wet/dry grinding
Applications: Powder blending, nanostructured alloy preparation, and material homogenization.
